PARQUET SCRAPING

Parquet scraping or old wooden floor scraping is carried out to quickly remove a large layer. This can be a thick layer of paint or varnish that has been applied repeatedly. Overall, this type of treatment is considered outdated. However, most often people refer to the process of parquet sanding as scraping.

When is Parquet Scraping Used?

  1. After parquet repair
    Scraping allows for relatively quick leveling of the floor. After using new parquet in certain areas, irregularities appear. In such cases, scraping is appropriate. And after scraping, a sanding machine will most likely need to be used.
  2. To remove layers of paint
    In this case, sanding a wooden floor increases the time and materials needed. Scraping allows for effective, and most importantly, quick removal of the necessary layer. In this case, scraping is a more effective method of preparation for sanding and varnishing.
